## Executive Summary

Governed procurement AI is most valuable when it fixes the operating foundation first.

- Large enterprises still run sourcing across fragmented ERP data, duplicate suppliers, spreadsheets, and delayed savings tracking.
- The best near-term value comes from data unification, supplier comparison support, approval routing, and savings follow-through.
- The strongest fit is indirect and mixed-spend procurement, not fully autonomous direct-material sourcing.

## Short Take

### What is broken

- Spend data is fragmented across ERP, AP, card, contract, and supplier systems.
- RFx setup and supplier comparison still consume too much sourcing time.
- Savings and compliance outcomes are often measured too late.

### What works

- Normalize procurement data before asking agents to act on it.
- Use AI to prepare, compare, summarize, and route work.
- Keep explicit human approval for supplier selection, exceptions, and savings booking.

### What value should show up

- 8% to 25% lower analyst and category-admin effort
- 10% to 35% faster sourcing cycles
- 5% to 20% more sourcing-event throughput
- 9 to 18 month payback potential for large enterprises

### What remains hard

- Audited ROI proof at scale
- Direct-material complexity
- Full closed-loop execution claims
